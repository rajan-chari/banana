What I like

The closure-state harness pattern (capturing statusChanges, idleReasons, logs on the controller's collaborators) is exactly the right testability shape — no spies, no mocks, just observed effects. That's the kind of test that survives controller refactors instead of breaking on them.

  • Tests observable behavior, not internal state. SessionHookController's permission-prompt test asserts the right invariant: "permission stays until a real-input keystroke," and proves it by feeding both an escape sequence (no-op) and a digit (clears). That's a real spec captured in a test, not a snapshot.
  • Fake-timer hygiene is right. afterEach(() => vi.useRealTimers()) at the top of session-checkpoint.test.ts closes the most common flake source. The stagger-offset test (lines 117–138) advances 999ms then 1ms across the boundary — that's the discipline I'd want.
  • Cost annotation is asserted at format level — $1.23. from costUsd: 1.234 verifies both the toFixed(2) call and the trailing period. Specific enough to catch a regression where someone reformats the prompt.
  • Highest-risk targets picked first. The two largest extracted controllers (187 + 178 lines) get focused coverage. Smart triage given limited time.
  • Dead-session no-op test (lines 137–146 of session-hooks) catches an entire class of bugs where a controller fires after teardown. Cheap and valuable.

Two nits, one small concern

Nit 1: log capture is mostly unused

Both harnesses collect logs: string[] but only the "full pending" check (line 151 of session-checkpoint.test.ts) asserts on log content. Everywhere else the array is wasted. Either drop the field from harnesses that don't use it, or add at least one assertion per controller verifying the log-on-error paths fire. Not blocking — just unused capture.

Nit 2: lastCheckpointTime assertion is weaker than it could be

session-checkpoint.test.ts:93 uses toBe(Date.now()) with fake timers set. It passes deterministically but would also pass if the controller stored a wrong-but-coincidentally-equal value. With vi.setSystemTime active, you could pin the exact expected value (new Date("...").getTime()) for a stricter contract. Trivial fix if you do another pass.

Concern: ws-runtime.ts still has no coverage

Of the three slices I originally sketched, you delivered 2 of 3 (controllers) and skipped both the WS round-trip and routes/admin preferences. The skip on preferences is well-justified (HOME dependency, deserves a proper fake — not a quick test). But ws-runtime.ts is 182 lines of new logic with timer-driven heartbeat, cleanup-on-close, and the broadcast helpers — none of which are exercised by anything today. That's the file most likely to harbor a silent regression. Not a refactor-blocker, but worth being the next test added rather than something deferred indefinitely.

Bottom line: tests are good — right spirit, right effort, right targets. Ship the refactor.

Treat the two nits as cleanup-on-next-pass. Treat ws-runtime coverage as the next concrete piece to land after this, not a hand-wave "later." It's where the next regression lives.
